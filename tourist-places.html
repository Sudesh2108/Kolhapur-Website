Famous Tourist-Places in Kolhapur

Mahalaxmi Temple

Ambabai Temple (also known as Mahalakshmi Mandir) is a Hindu temple dedicated to Adi Shakti

New Palace

It is dedicated to a fine collection of possessions of Chhatrapatis of Kolhapur like costumes, weapons, games, jewellery, embroidery and paraphernalia such as silver elephant saddles.

Rankala

This historical lake is a treasure trove for scenery admirers.

Kopeshwar Temple

Kopeshwar Temple is at Khidrapur, Kolhapur district, Maharashtra. It is a Hindu temple dedicated to Shiva.

Panhala Fort

It is one of the largest forts in the Deccan, with a perimeter of 14 km (9 mi) and 110 lookout posts. It is 845 m (2,772 ft) above sea level.

Jotiba Temple

Jyotiba Temple (Marathi: ज्योतिबा) is a holy site of Hinduism near Wadi Ratnagiri in Kolhapur district of Maharashtra state in western India.

Ram-Linga Caves

Ancient and beautiful cave temple from the Ramayana period and is famous for the myth that Lord Rama resided here during his fourteen-year exile.

Shalini Palace

Shalini Palace ( Shaalini ) in the city of Kolhapur in the Indian state of Maharashtra was built in 1931–34 at a cost of Rs. 800,000 and was named after Princess Shalini (Shaalini) Raje of Kolhapur.

Bhavani Mandap

It is a royal palace of the Maharaja of Kolhapur. It used to be a durbar of the Chhatrapati Maharaja.

Masai Plateau

is known for scenery and various types of seasonal wildflowers that bloom in the months of August and September.
